Deep tech commercialization · 02

Not all funding is the same — some costs you equity, some just costs you time

Know which type of capital fits your stage, and never give away equity before you have to.

Dilutive funding

You give up ownership

Angel investment$25K–$500K, pre-seed, high risk tolerance
Venture capitalSeed to Series A+, milestone-driven
Convertible notes / SAFEsDebt that converts to equity at next round
University spin-out equityTech transfer office takes a stake

Non-dilutive funding

You keep full ownership

SBIR / STTR grantsNSF, NIH, DOD — up to $2M+, no equity
Federal grants (R01, etc.)Academic-route, PI-led, longer timelines
State & foundation grantsGa. Research Alliance, disease foundations
Revenue / contractsBest non-dilutive capital: paying customers

The key insight

Deep tech founders often raise VC too early. SBIR Phase I (~$275K) and Phase II (~$1.8M) can get you to clinical proof-of-concept with zero dilution — dramatically increasing your valuation before your first equity round.
