Patents protect your moat — but only if you file at the right time and understand what you own

Three concepts every researcher-founder needs before talking to a tech transfer office or investor.

Provisional

Provisional patent

Establishes a priority date. Gives you 12 months to refine the invention before filing a full application. Lower cost, faster to file.

~$1,500–$3K with attorney

Non-provisional

Non-provisional patent

The real patent. Examined by USPTO. Can take 2–4 years to grant. Defines the actual claims you can enforce.

$10K–$30K+ prosecution cost

FTO

Freedom to operate

A legal analysis confirming your product doesn't infringe someone else's active patents. Separate from owning a patent yourself.

$5K–$20K per analysis

The key insight

Owning a patent does not mean you have the right to sell your product — someone else's patent might block you. Always run an FTO analysis before commercializing, separate from your own IP strategy.
