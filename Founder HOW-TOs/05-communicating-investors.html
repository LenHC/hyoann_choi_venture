Deep tech commercialization · 05

Investors fund people and markets first — your technology is third

Every investor meeting is really answering six questions. Know them before you walk in.

01

What problem are you solving and how big is it?

Lead with patient burden or market size — not your mechanism of action.

02

Why are you and your team uniquely able to solve it?

Lab expertise + a clinical or commercial co-founder is a stronger signal than solo academic.

03

What's your regulatory and reimbursement path?

Deep tech investors expect you to know your FDA class and whether Medicare covers it.

04

What milestone does this round get you to?

Be specific: "510(k) clearance" or "Phase I safety data" — not "further development."

05

What does your IP position look like?

Provisional filed, licensed from GT, or owned outright — and what's the FTO status?

06

What's the exit path and for whom?

Name 3–5 strategic acquirers and why they'd buy you — medtech, pharma, or digital health.

The key insight

Researchers often over-explain the science and under-explain the business. Investors already assume the technology is interesting — they need to believe the market is real, the team can execute, and the timing is right.

Do

Lead with the patient or customer problem

Know your comparable exits (comps)

Have a specific ask: amount + use of funds

Send a one-pager before the meeting

Don't

Open with "our technology can..."

Say the market is "everyone who has X disease"

Dismiss competition ("we have no competitors")

Ask for an NDA before sharing your deck
