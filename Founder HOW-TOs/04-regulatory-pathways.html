Your FDA pathway determines your timeline, your data requirements, and your fundraise

Picking the wrong pathway costs years. Know which class your device falls into before you design your clinical strategy.

510(k)

Substantial equivalence

Class II device

Timeline3–12 months
Clinical dataOften not required
RequirementPredicate device exists
ExampleBlood pressure monitor
De Novo

Novel low-to-mod risk

Class I / II (new)

Timeline12–24 months
Clinical dataUsually required
RequirementNo predicate exists
ExampleFirst AI diagnostic tool
PMA

Premarket approval

Class III device

Timeline3–7 years
Clinical dataRequired (pivotal trial)
RequirementHigh risk / life-sustaining
ExampleImplantable cardiac device

The key insight

Investors price your regulatory risk. A 510(k) path with a clear predicate is fundable at pre-seed. A PMA path requires Series A+ capital and a clinical team before most VCs will lead. Know your class on day one.

Also relevant for therapeutics

IND → NDA / BLA

Drug / biologic pathway

Phase I–III trials required. 10–15 year timeline typical. Breakthrough designation can accelerate.

Combination products

Device + drug hybrids

Regulated by primary mode of action. Often requires both FDA centers (CDRH + CDER) coordination.
